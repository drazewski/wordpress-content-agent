## Universal editorial style

Goal: write articles that are clear, credible, readable, and adaptable. Do not imitate one fixed author voice unless the user asks for that explicitly.

Always start from the project context collected through `wp-onboarding`. Match the tone, audience, format, and voice defined there before drafting.

## Tone

- Prefer a natural, factual, and confident tone.
- Avoid empty intensity, inflated praise, and pseudo-expert language.
- Adjust formality to the audience:
  - more explanatory for general readers,
  - more precise for specialist audiences,
  - more practical for guide-style content.

## Structure

### Introduction

- Enter the topic quickly.
- Explain what the article is about and why the topic matters.
- Do not open with worn-out rhetorical questions or school-style generalities.
- Keep the introduction short and purposeful.

### Main body

- Use `H2` and `H3` headings to separate ideas.
- Let each section move the article forward.
- Put the most important facts, data, or arguments in the middle sections.
- Use examples only when they clarify the point.

### Ending

- Close the topic cleanly.
- Highlight the main takeaway, implication, or unresolved tension.
- Avoid generic ending formulas like "in conclusion" if they add nothing.

## What to avoid

- filler,
- obvious statements,
- copy that sounds auto-generated,
- keyword stuffing,
- overexplaining simple points,
- forced lists where paragraphs work better.

## WordPress-ready format

Return the article in this structure:

- `title`
- `excerpt`
- `slug`
- `tags`
- `category`
- `metaDescription`
- `articleHtml` or `articleMarkdown`
